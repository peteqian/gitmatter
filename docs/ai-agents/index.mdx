gitmatter is built to be driven by an AI assistant you already use. You connect a
tool like **Claude Desktop**, **Claude web/CLI**, or **ChatGPT** to gitmatter once.
After that, you ask in plain language — _"review these NDAs"_ — and the assistant does
the work inside gitmatter. You stay in the AI tool you like. gitmatter does the legal
work behind the scenes and keeps a full record of it.

Here is the important part: **anything the assistant does is recorded just like work
done by a person.** It is logged under the assistant's name, in the same history as
everyone else's work. Connecting an assistant is not a hidden back door that skips
this record.

## How it connects (in plain words)

AI tools connect to outside services using a shared standard called **MCP**
(Model Context Protocol). Think of it as a common language that lets your AI tool plug
into other services. gitmatter speaks this language at `/api/mcp`. To connect, you
point your AI tool at gitmatter's address and sign in. The next page walks you through
it.
