title: What agents can do
description: The work an AI assistant can do for you, and how every action stays on the record.
---

Once connected, the AI assistant can do real work in gitmatter — the same product
features you use yourself. But it can **never** touch your account's security. This
page explains both sides in plain terms. For the exact list of tools, see the
[MCP tools reference](/api-reference/mcp-tools).

## What the assistant can do

- **Reviews** — list, create, and run table-style reviews, and read the cells and
  their citations.
- **Documents** — read a document, suggest tracked changes, accept or reject them, and
  produce a Word (`.docx`) file.
- **Workflows** — list, read, and write workflows.
- **Clients &amp; matters** — list and create clients and matters, and list a matter's
  documents.
- **Search &amp; fetch** — search across your reviews and documents and pull up their
  full content.
- **Legal research** — search US case law and check citations, where the matter's
  jurisdiction allows it.
- **History** — read the change history, see what changed field by field, and find out
  who last set a value.

## What the assistant can never do

The assistant **cannot** change your login or your settings. It cannot read or change
your AI provider keys, it cannot create or cancel tokens, and it cannot change your
account. All of that stays with you, inside the app.

## Everything is on the record

Every action the assistant takes is written to the same history as work done by a
person, and is clearly **recorded and labeled as done by the AI** (for example, "via
Claude Desktop"). That means:

- You can see exactly what the assistant did, value by value.
- You can trace any change back to the assistant and the exact moment it happened.
- The secret code (token) the assistant used is recorded too, so a firm can see and
  measure usage for each connection.

This is what makes "bring your own assistant" safe: you get the convenience of the AI
tool you already like, without giving up the record.
