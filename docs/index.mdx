gitmatter is a tool for legal teams. It helps you review contracts, pull
information out of documents, draft new documents, and ask questions about your
files. An AI assistant can help with all of this, or you can do the work yourself.

What makes gitmatter different is simple. **Every change is written down and kept.**
It does not matter if a person made the change in the app or an AI assistant made it
for you. gitmatter always keeps a complete record of who changed what, when, and why.
Nothing is lost, and nothing happens quietly in the background. You can always look
back and see exactly how a document or a piece of data got to where it is today.

## What you can do

- **Review contracts.** gitmatter suggests edits as tracked changes, the same way
  "track changes" works in a word processor. You read each suggestion and decide to
  accept it or reject it.
- **Pull information into a table.** Ask gitmatter to read a stack of documents and
  fill in a table for you. For example, you could ask for the governing law, the term
  length, and the renewal date of every vendor contract in a matter, and then export
  that table.
- **Draft documents.** gitmatter can create a Word document for you, following a set
  of steps you have saved.
- **Ask questions.** Chat about your documents in plain language. The answers point
  you to the exact place in the source document they came from, so you can check them.
- **Use your own AI assistant.** If you already use a tool like Claude or ChatGPT, you
  can connect it to gitmatter. It can then do this work for you safely, and every
  step it takes is still written down.

## A note on trust

gitmatter is built so you can trust the result enough to file it, and so that anyone
reviewing your work later can trace every change back to the person or AI assistant
who made it. Answers always show their sources, and nothing happens off the record.
