The **assistant** lets you ask questions in plain, everyday language and get answers
that are based on your own documents. You can ask about a whole matter (everything
filed under it), or you can narrow in on one or two specific documents.

Think of it as asking a very fast colleague who has read every page for you.

## Answers you can check

When the assistant gives you an answer, it also shows you where it found it. Every
answer comes with a link back to the exact spot in the source document. So you can
click straight through and read that passage yourself.

This is on purpose. You should never have to take an answer on trust. You stay in
control: read the source, confirm it is right, and only then rely on it.

## What the assistant can do for you

The assistant does more than answer questions. It can also do real work for you. For
example, it can suggest edits to a contract, fill in a review table, run one of your
saved workflows, or produce a finished Word document.

When it does any of this, the action is written down, the same way it would be for a
person, and clearly marked as the assistant's work. That way the full record of who
did what stays complete and honest.

## Your firm's own AI

The chat runs on your firm's own account with an AI provider (such as Claude, Gemini,
OpenAI, or OpenRouter). Your administrator sets this up. You can pick which AI model
to use for each message you send.

For how this usage is counted and kept within budget, see
[Spending controls](/admin/spending-controls).

## Bringing in an outside assistant

Separately, you can connect a tool you already use day to day (such as Claude Desktop
or ChatGPT) and let _it_ work with your gitcounsel matters for you. That is explained
in [AI &amp; agents](/ai-agents).
