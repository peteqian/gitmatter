gitcounsel keeps a complete record of every change made to your work. Nothing is ever
quietly erased. This page explains what that means for you in everyday practice, and
why it gives you peace of mind.

## Every change is written down

Whenever something changes — an edit to a contract, a single piece of information in a
review table, an update to a workflow — a new entry is added to the record. The old
version is never wiped out. Instead, the new version is simply added on top, so the
whole story stays intact.

Each entry captures four things:

- **Who** made the change — a named member of your team, or the AI assistant acting on
  someone's behalf.
- **What** changed — the exact value before, and the exact value after.
- **When** it happened — the date and time.
- A short **note** describing the change, if one was given.

## Looking through the history

From any document, review table, or workflow, you can open its history and see the
list of changes laid out in order, newest or oldest first. You can open any single
change to see its full before-and-after detail, side by side.

## Tracing one value back to its source

Sometimes you do not want the whole history. You want the answer to one simple, urgent
question about a single piece of information: "Where did this come from? Who set it,
and when?"

Picture this. A renewal date is sitting in your review table, and you are about to rely
on it in advice to a client. Before you do, you want to know exactly how that date got
there. gitcounsel can trace that one value straight back to the exact change that set
it — telling you who set it and when. It is the fastest way to settle a question like
"Where did this renewal date come from?" without scrolling through everything else.

## Why this matters to you

This complete record is what lets you stand behind gitcounsel's output. You can file it
with confidence, because you can always show your working. If a client, a partner, or
an auditor ever asks how a result was reached, the answer is right there.

<Callout type="info">
  Nothing happens off the record — and that includes work done by the AI assistant. Its changes
  appear in the very same history as everyone else's, clearly marked as made by the assistant. There
  is no hidden activity to worry about.
</Callout>
