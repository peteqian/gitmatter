This page gets you going in gitmatter from a standing start. It assumes no technical
knowledge — if you can use email and a word processor, you can use gitmatter. Read it
top to bottom once, and you will have done the main things the app is for.

<Callout type="info">
  Someone in your firm (usually IT) sets gitmatter up once. You do not need to install anything. You
  just sign in with the email and password they give you.
</Callout>

## The big idea

Think of gitmatter as a document system with a perfect memory. Every time something
changes — a contract is edited, a box in a table is filled in, a set of saved steps is
updated — gitmatter writes down a record of that change. Each record tells you:

- **Who** made the change — a named person on your team, or an AI assistant acting for
  someone.
- **What** changed — the exact wording before and after.
- **When** it happened — the date and time.
- **Why** it happened — a short note, if one was given.

Because of this, you never lose your history. You can trace any word, number, or date
back to the moment it was set, and see who set it. If you have used "track changes" in a
word processor, this is the same idea, applied to everything in the product.

## How your work is organized

gitmatter follows the way a firm already files its work. There are three layers:

1. **Client** — the company or person you act for.
2. **Matter** — one case or project for that client, such as "Acme acquisition". This is
   where your day-to-day work happens.
3. **Your work** — documents, reviews, tables, and saved steps, all kept inside a matter.

So the path is always **Client → Matter → your work**. Anyone added to a matter can see
and work on everything inside it, and every action they take is saved under their name.

## Your first ten minutes

Follow these steps once and you will have done the main things gitmatter is for.

1. **Sign in** with the email and password your administrator gave you. You land on the
   list of matters you are part of.
2. **Open or add a client.** Go to the **Clients** screen. Pick the client you act for,
   or add a new one.
3. **Open or add a matter** under that client — for example, "Office lease renewal". This
   is the folder your work lives in.
4. **Upload a document.** Inside the matter, add a contract (PDF or Word). gitmatter reads
   it for you in the background; its status changes when it is ready.
5. **Ask a question.** Open the assistant and ask in plain words, such as "What is the
   renewal date?" The answer comes with a link to the exact spot in the document, so you
   can check it.
6. **Review suggested edits.** If the assistant or a colleague proposes changes (a
   redline), look at each one and **accept** or **reject** it, one at a time.
7. **Look at the history.** Open any document or table's history to see who changed what,
   and when.

That is the whole loop: organize the work, bring in documents, ask and review, and trust
the record.

## Words you will see

A short glossary of the terms used across gitmatter:

- **Client** — the company or person you act for.
- **Matter** — one case or project for a client; where your work lives.
- **Jurisdiction** — the place whose laws apply to a matter, such as a US state. Setting
  it correctly switches on the right tools (like case-law research).
- **Redline** — suggested edits shown as tracked changes, which you accept or reject.
- **Citation** — the exact spot in a source document that an answer came from.
- **Cell** — one box in a review table, where a row (document) meets a column (question).
- **Workflow** — a saved set of steps you can run again and again.
- **Assistant** — the built-in AI you chat with inside gitmatter.
